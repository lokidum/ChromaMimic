# LUT Builder, service and SEO plan

The tool is the lead magnet. The strategy is the same one lutbuilder.ai and fylm.ai run: a free in-browser utility that ranks for high-intent colour-grading searches, then converts a slice of that traffic into paid features or into Nunik Co. service work. This plan covers the positioning, the model, and a search content cluster to make the service findable rather than dependent on luck.

## Positioning

One sentence: build a matching LUT from any two frames, free, in your browser, nothing uploaded. The "nothing uploaded" line is a real differentiator against the AI tools that require an account and a server round-trip, and it doubles as a trust and privacy hook for working professionals.

Where it fits Nunik Co.: it is a credibility piece and a top-of-funnel net. A filmmaker who finds the free tool, gets a clean result, and sees it was built by Nunik Co. is a warm lead for paid web work, automation, or a custom tool build. It also slots into the ICP3 founder-brand and creative audiences without competing with the ICP1 trades focus.

## Model

- **Free tier:** 17³ and 33³ export, all four methods, smoothing, black/white normalisation. No login.
- **Paid or gated tier (phase 2):** 65³ export, log colour space presets with built-in CST guidance, batch a folder of frames, save and name LUT libraries, and shareable links. Either a small one-off, a low monthly, or email-gated for the free pro features.
- **Service upsell:** "want a custom grade or a branded LUT pack for your studio? Nunik Co. builds these." A contact path on the tool page feeding the pipeline.

## SEO content cluster

Audience: filmmakers, videographers, content creators, and junior colourists working in DaVinci Resolve, Adobe Premiere Pro, and Final Cut Pro, plus the large wedding and event shooter segment grading Sony, Canon and Panasonic log footage.

### Topic details

#### 1. How to Create a LUT From a Reference Image
- **Keyword:** create lut from image
- **Intent:** informational, high commercial adjacency
- **Funnel:** TOFU, the pillar
- **Type:** comprehensive guide with the tool embedded
- **Difficulty:** medium
- **Why:** this is the exact phrase someone types when they want what the tool does. Owning it is the whole game.
- **Angle:** a genuinely free, no-login, in-browser walkthrough that ends with the reader holding a .cube, versus competitors who gate the result behind signup.
- **Key sections:** what a reference-matched LUT is, picking good frames, the build steps, applying it in each NLE, when matching fails (log).

#### 2. What Is a .cube LUT File (and How to Use One)
- **Keyword:** what is a cube lut
- **Intent:** informational
- **Funnel:** TOFU
- **Type:** explainer
- **Difficulty:** low
- **Why:** entry-level query with steady volume, easy to rank, feeds the pillar.
- **Angle:** plain-language, no jargon, with a peek inside an actual .cube file so it stops being a black box.
- **Key sections:** the lookup-table idea, 1D vs 3D, common sizes (17/33/65) and compatibility, where each NLE loads them.

#### 3. Match a Film Look From Any Screenshot, Free
- **Keyword:** match film look lut
- **Intent:** commercial
- **Funnel:** MOFU
- **Type:** tool landing page plus short guide
- **Difficulty:** medium
- **Why:** captures the "I saw a look I love and want it" desire, which is the emotional driver behind the whole category.
- **Angle:** worked example matching a recognisable cinematic still, honest about what a global match can and cannot do.
- **Key sections:** the use case, the three-minute workflow, realistic expectations, download.

#### 4. Why Your LUT Looks Flat on S-Log3 Footage
- **Keyword:** lut looks flat slog3
- **Intent:** informational
- **Funnel:** TOFU
- **Type:** problem-solution explainer
- **Difficulty:** low
- **Why:** extremely common pain point, strong intent, and it positions the tool as made by people who actually understand colour pipelines.
- **Angle:** the colour space transform step nearly every beginner misses, mapped to Resolve, Premiere and FCP.
- **Key sections:** what log is, why a Rec.709 LUT mangles it, the CST node order, the scope sanity check.

#### 5. Free LUT Generator vs fylm.ai vs LUTBuilder.ai
- **Keyword:** best lut generator
- **Intent:** commercial
- **Funnel:** MOFU
- **Type:** comparison
- **Difficulty:** medium
- **Why:** comparison queries convert well and let the tool stand next to the names people already search.
- **Angle:** honest table including price, login requirement, privacy (local vs cloud), export sizes, and method transparency. Win on free, private, and no-login.
- **Key sections:** criteria, the table, who each tool suits, verdict.

#### 6 to 8. Install / apply LUT in Resolve, Premiere, Final Cut
- **Keywords:** install lut davinci resolve, apply lut premiere pro, custom lut final cut
- **Intent:** informational
- **Funnel:** TOFU
- **Type:** short how-to with screenshots
- **Difficulty:** low
- **Why:** evergreen, high-volume, low-competition utility queries. Every one ends with a natural link back to the tool.
- **Angle:** each one includes the log/CST caveat so the LUT actually lands, which most thin how-tos skip.
- **Key sections:** exact menu path, where the LUT folder lives, the CST reminder, troubleshooting "it will not load" (size 65 caveat).

#### 9. 7 Mistakes That Ruin Your LUTs
- **Keyword:** lut mistakes
- **Intent:** informational
- **Funnel:** TOFU
- **Type:** listicle
- **Difficulty:** low
- **Why:** shareable, links to half the cluster, quick to produce.
- **Angle:** real mistakes (wrong colour space, milky blacks, oversized LUTs, matching mismatched frames) each tied to a fix the tool already handles.
- **Key sections:** one mistake per heading, each with the fix and an internal link.

#### 10. Colour Matching Two Shots: A Practical Guide
- **Keyword:** colour match shots
- **Intent:** informational
- **Funnel:** MOFU
- **Type:** deep dive
- **Difficulty:** medium
- **Why:** broadens the tool from "film look" into the everyday editing problem of making two cameras or two angles agree.
- **Angle:** when to match by LUT versus by hand, and the limits of a global match on dissimilar framing.
- **Key sections:** the problem, frame selection, building the match, when to fall back to manual.

### Recommended publishing order

1. What Is a .cube LUT File, foundation, easiest to rank, defines terms the rest reuse.
2. How to Create a LUT From a Reference Image, the pillar, embeds the tool.
3. Install a LUT in DaVinci Resolve, quick win, high volume.
4. Why Your LUT Looks Flat on S-Log3, authority builder, strong pain point.
5. Apply a LUT in Premiere Pro, then Use a Custom LUT in Final Cut Pro.
6. 7 Mistakes That Ruin Your LUTs, hub that links everything.
7. Match a Film Look From Any Screenshot, commercial conversion page.
8. Colour Matching Two Shots, then the comparison piece once you have traffic to convert.

### Internal linking plan

- Pillar (1) links to every supporting article and to the tool.
- The three NLE how-tos (6 to 8) each link up to the pillar and across to the S-Log3 piece (4).
- The mistakes listicle (9) links to 2, 4, and the comparison (5).
- The comparison (5) and the film-look page (3) both link to the pillar and carry the primary tool call to action.
- Every article ends with a soft Nunik Co. service line for custom grades and LUT packs.

## Quick wins to make the service itself more foolproof

- Put the tool on its own clean URL with the pillar guide on the same page, so the ranking page and the utility are one thing.
- Add a visible privacy line ("runs in your browser, nothing is uploaded") near the upload zones, it is a real trust and conversion lever.
- Ship the three NLE how-tos before launch so day-one visitors can actually finish the job.
- Capture emails only in exchange for the pro features, not as a wall in front of the core free result.
